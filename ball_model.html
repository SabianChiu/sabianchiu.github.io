Brain Reading Drink Dispenser

The Goal
With a team of three other students, our goal was to design and build a "futuristic" vending machine that would take user electroencephalogram (EEG) readings and dispense a cocktail with the mix concentration determined by the level of user concentration. We built this machine from scratch to participate in the UBC E-Week 2020 Ball Model competition, where departments compete by creating projects that best represent their engineering discipline... while somehow incorporating alcohol.

Functionality
This machine uses a Muse EEG device that detects the electrical activity in the user's brain and analyzes the user's level of concentration. This data is fed into an Arduino processor, which is programmed to take the numerical average of the EEG readings over a period of ten seconds, and use that to calculate the type and amount of mixer to add to a preset amount (1oz) of liquor. Using a 4:1 ratio of liquor-to-mixer as the lower limit of mixer dispensed, the greater concentration the user exhibits, the greater the amount of mixer that is dispensed and mixed into the liquor. Connected to the Arduino board output pins are five servo motors we purchased from a local electronics store. This servo has been mounted to the bottom of an internal panel just below the display box that essentially pinches tubes (from the IV bags storing the drinks) at their default position and rotates to un-pinch the tubes to allow for fluid flow by gravity.
